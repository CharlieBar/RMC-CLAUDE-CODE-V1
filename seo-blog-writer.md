# SEO Blog Writer Guide

Create blog content that ranks in search, provides genuine value to patients, and guides readers toward booking appointments. This guide covers topic selection, article structure, SEO optimization, and conversion elements.

---

## Blog Content Strategy

### Content Pillars for Rand Medical Center

| Pillar | Topics | Target Audience |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Pain Conditions** | Back pain, sciatica, neck pain, joint pain | People experiencing pain, researching causes |
| **Treatment Education** | What to expect, preparation, recovery | People considering treatment options |
| **Prevention & Wellness** | Exercises, ergonomics, lifestyle tips | Proactive health seekers |
| **Injury Guides** | Auto accidents, work injuries, sports | Recently injured, need guidance |
| **Patient Stories** | Testimonials, recovery journeys | People seeking reassurance |

### Topic Selection Criteria

**Good blog topics:**
- Answer questions people actually search
- Have reasonable search volume (100-10,000/month)
- Match a stage in the patient journey
- Connect to services you offer
- Aren't already covered by major medical sites (Mayo, WebMD)

**Topic research sources:**
- Google "People Also Ask" boxes
- Google Autocomplete suggestions
- Patient questions from front desk
- Keyword research tools (Semrush, Ahrefs)
- Reddit/Quora medical questions

## Article Types & Templates

### 1. How-To/Guide Articles
**Best for:** Actionable advice, self-care tips, preparation guides

**Title formats:**
- "How to [Achieve Goal] with [Condition]"
- "The Complete Guide to [Topic]"
- "[Number] Ways to [Relieve/Prevent/Manage] [Problem]"

**Structure:**
```
H1: How to [Achieve Goal]

Introduction (100-150 words)
- Hook: Relatable problem
- Promise: What reader will learn
- Credibility: Why trust this advice

H2: Understanding [Topic] (background)

H2: [Number] [Ways/Steps/Tips] to [Goal]
  H3: 1. [First tip]
  H3: 2. [Second tip]
  H3: 3. [Third tip]
  ...

H2: When to See a Doctor

H2: How We Can Help (soft sell)

H2: FAQs

Conclusion + CTA
```

### 2. Condition Deep-Dives
**Best for:** Educational content, building topical authority

**Title formats:**
- "What You Need to Know About [Condition]"
- "[Condition]: Causes, Symptoms, and Treatment Options"
- "Understanding [Condition]: A Complete Guide"

**Structure:**
```
H1: [Condition]: What You Need to Know

Introduction
- What is condition (brief)
- Who it affects
- Why it matters

H2: What Causes [Condition]?

H2: Recognizing [Condition] Symptoms

H2: When Symptoms Are Serious (red flags)

H2: Treatment Options
  H3: Home Remedies
  H3: Medical Treatments
  H3: When Surgery Is Needed

H2: Living With [Condition]

H2: Prevention Tips

H2: FAQs

Conclusion + CTA
```

### 3. Comparison/Versus Articles
**Best for:** Commercial intent, people deciding between options

**Title formats:**
- "[Option A] vs [Option B]: Which Is Right for You?"
- "[Treatment A] or [Treatment B]? How to Choose"
- "Comparing [Options] for [Condition]"

**Structure:**
```
H1: [Option A] vs [Option B]: Making the Right Choice

Introduction
- The decision readers face
- Why it matters
- Quick answer preview

H2: What Is [Option A]?
  - How it works
  - Best for
  - Pros and cons

H2: What Is [Option B]?
  - How it works
  - Best for
  - Pros and cons

H2: [Option A] vs [Option B]: Key Differences
  - Comparison table
  - Detailed breakdown

H2: How to Choose the Right Option

H2: FAQs

Conclusion + CTA
```

### 4. Listicles
**Best for:** Scannable content, social sharing, featured snippets

**Title formats:**
- "[Number] [Things] Every [Audience] Should Know About [Topic]"
- "[Number] Signs You May Have [Condition]"
- "[Number] [Exercises/Tips/Foods] for [Goal]"

**Structure:**
```
H1: [Number] [Topic Items]

Introduction (brief)

H2: 1. [First Item]
  - Explanation
  - Why it matters
  - Actionable tip

H2: 2. [Second Item]
  ...

H2: When to Seek Professional Help

Conclusion + CTA
```

## Writing Guidelines

### The Hook (First 100 Words)

**Start with the reader's reality:**
```
GOOD: "You're lying in bed at 2 AM, unable to sleep because of the burning pain
shooting down your leg. Sound familiar? You're not alone..."

BAD: "Sciatica is a common condition that affects many people. In this article,
we will discuss..."
```

**Hook formulas:**
1. **Pain point opener**: Start with the problem they're experiencing
2. **Surprising statistic**: "Did you know 80% of adults experience back pain?"
3. **Question opener**: "What if the solution to your back pain was simpler than you thought?"
4. **Story opener**: "Last week, a patient walked into our office unable to stand straight..."

### Body Content Rules

**Paragraph structure:**
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- One idea per paragraph
- Use transition words between sections

**Readability:**
- 8th-9th grade reading level
- Short sentences (under 20 words average)
- Active voice preferred
- Explain medical terms when first used

**Engagement techniques:**
- Ask rhetorical questions
- Use "you" language
- Include relatable examples
- Break up text with formatting

### The Close (Last 200 Words)

**Summary + Action:**

## SEO Optimization

### Keyword Placement

| Location | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **H1** | "5 Stretches That Help Relieve Sciatica Pain" |
| **First paragraph** | "If you're dealing with sciatica pain, these stretches can help..." |
| **At least 2 H2s** | "H2: Best Stretches for Sciatica Relief" |
| **Image alt text** | "woman doing piriformis stretch for sciatica pain" |
| **Meta description** | "Learn 5 effective stretches for sciatica pain relief you can do at home..." |

### Internal Links (5-8 per article)

**Link to:**
- Related condition page (1-2 links)
- Relevant treatment pages (2-3 links)
- Related blog posts (1-2 links)
- Doctor profile (1 link)
- Main service page (1 link)

**Anchor text examples:**
- "epidural steroid injections can provide relief"
- "learn more about our sciatica treatment options"
- "Dr. Chunduri, our pain management specialist"

### External Links (1-2 per article)

**Link to:**
- NIH, Mayo Clinic, medical associations
- Published research studies
- Government health resources (.gov)

**Purpose:** Builds credibility, supports claims with authoritative sources

## Formatting Best Practices

### Use These Elements

**Bullet points for:**
- Symptoms lists
- Benefits
- Tips
- Features

**Numbered lists for:**
- Step-by-step instructions
- Rankings
- Processes

**Tables for:**
- Comparisons
- Data presentation
- Treatment options

**Bold text for:**
- Key terms
- Important warnings
- Scannable highlights

**Block quotes for:**
- Patient testimonials
- Expert quotes
- Important callouts

### Visual Content

**Every article should have:**
- Featured image (relevant, high-quality)
- 2-3 images throughout (exercises, diagrams, illustrations)
- Consider: infographic, video embed, chart

**Image requirements:**
- Alt text with keywords
- Compressed for fast loading
- Relevant to content
- Professional quality

## Content Calendar Recommendations

**Publishing frequency:** 2-4 posts per month

**Content mix:**
- 2 educational/informational posts
- 1 treatment-focused post
- 1 seasonal/timely post (injury prevention, flu season, etc.)

**Seasonal topics:**
- January: New Year wellness, resolution injuries
- Spring: Sports injury prevention, allergy season
- Summer: Heat-related issues, outdoor activity injuries
- Fall: Back-to-school physicals, sports injuries
- Winter: Slip and fall prevention, cold weather pain

**Evergreen topics to prioritize:**
- Condition explanations (sciatica, back pain, arthritis)
- Treatment guides (injections, physical therapy)
- Self-care tips (exercises, ergonomics)
- "When to see a doctor" guides
