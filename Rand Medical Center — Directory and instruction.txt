2) URL & Metadata Conventions

Domain: https://www.randmedicalcenter.com/ (adjust if different)

Slug style: lowercase, hyphenated; avoid dates in URLs; stable, descriptive (e.g., /services/pain-management/radiofrequency-ablation/).

Title pattern: <Primary Intent Keyword> in Arlington Heights, IL | Rand Medical Center.

Meta description: 150–160 chars; begin with benefit/intent, include city, service, and unique value (Joint Commission‑accredited surgical facility, on‑site X‑ray/labs, same‑day care, etc.).

Canonical: set per page; avoid duplicate category/tag indexes.

Breadcrumbs: Home → Services → Pain Management → Radiofrequency Ablation using BreadcrumbList schema.

Schema.org (JSON‑LD):

Sitewide: MedicalClinic/LocalBusiness (name, logo, phone, hours, geo, sameAs).

Providers: Physician with medicalSpecialty & affiliation.

Services: Service or MedicalProcedure/TherapeuticProcedure (add howPerformed, prep, recovery, risks as text).

Conditions: MedicalCondition (symptoms, possibleTreatment).

FAQ sections: FAQPage.

3) High‑Level Silo Architecture

A. Services (What We Do) → B. Conditions (What You Have) → C. Treatments & Procedures (How We Treat) → D. Patients & Resources → E. Providers → F. About/Location/Contact → G. Blog/Guides

Interlinking rules:

Every Condition links to 2–4 relevant Treatments + 1–2 Service pillars + related Blog guides.

Every Treatment links back to Conditions it treats + parent Service pillar + Surgery Center when applicable.

Service pillars list featured conditions & procedures, location CTA, and PT/rehab pathways.

Blog posts cross‑link to condition/treatment pages and the Book Now CTA.
